<section class="home-philosophy" id="philosophy">
  <header class="home-section-header">
    <p class="home-section-kicker">READ<br>MY PHILOSOPHY</p>
  </header>
  <div class="home-philosophy-text">
    <p>"In the Bahamas, we say the sea teaches patience, and the sun teaches joy."</p>
    <p>
      I grew up where the tide does not rush for anyone. You either learn patience or you learn pain.
    </p>
    <p>
      My philosophy was shaped by these islands. Here, every breeze carries conversation. Every wave reminds you that
      movement is survival. Wisdom does not grow in chaos. It grows in good soil. My soil is Bahamian, resilient,
      rhythmic, stubborn in the best way.
    </p>
    <p>
      From straw markets to regattas, from dockside debates to boardroom strategy, I study patterns. Like a conch diver
      searching blue water, I go deeper than the surface. I question assumptions. I challenge inherited thinking. I
      welcome better ideas when they prove themselves.
    </p>
    <p>This Almanack is that search, written down.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="home-philosophy-meta">

  </div>

  <div class="home-pillars">
    <article class="home-pillar">
      <h3><span class="home-pillar-number">01 ·</span> Rooted — Bahamian by Design</h3>
      <p>
        Not despite where I am from. Because of it.
      </p>
      <p>
        The islands trained me early. Community before capital. Patience before panic. Build something that lasts,
        not something that trends.
      </p>
      <p>
        Small nations can produce large ideas when they own their tools. That belief is not theory to me. It is practice.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="home-pillar">
      <h3><span class="home-pillar-number">02 ·</span> Curious — Always Looking Deeper</h3>
      <p>
        Curiosity is not a personality trait. It is a discipline.
      </p>
      <p>
        I do not rush to answers. I sit with the tension of a problem until it reveals its structure. Most of these
        essays began as a question that would not leave me alone.
      </p>
      <p>If something feels incomplete, I pull the thread.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="home-pillar">
      <h3><span class="home-pillar-number">03 ·</span> Shared — Knowledge is Infrastructure</h3>
      <p>
        Information locked in private vaults does not build nations.
      </p>
      <p>Shared knowledge does.</p>
      <p>
        If I have learned something useful, it belongs in circulation. Every page here is a bridge, so the next builder
        does not have to start from scratch.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="home-pillar">
      <h3><span class="home-pillar-number">04 ·</span> Joyful — Joy in the Journey</h3>
      <p>
        Serious work does not require a serious face.
      </p>
      <p>
        Happiness is learned. Chosen. Practiced. I find it in small wins, sharp conversations, clean code, a good story,
        a fresh breeze off the water.
      </p>
      <p>
        That is the Bahamian way. And it belongs in any philosophy worth keeping.
      </p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
